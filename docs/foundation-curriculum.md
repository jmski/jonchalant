# The Foundation — Course Curriculum

**Target:** 200+ hours of content across 8 modules
**Format:** Self-paced video course teaching executive presence through the lens of dance
**Audience:** Introverts, busy professionals, emerging leaders who want to command a room without performing

---

## Course Philosophy

Soft skills aren't soft — they're the hardest skills to teach because they live in the body, not in a textbook. Dance is the most direct way to develop presence, body control, active listening, improvisation, reciprocation, and tonality because dance *is* all of those things in motion.

The Foundation doesn't teach you to dance. It uses dance as the vehicle to rewire how you show up — in meetings, on stage, in conversations, in life.

---

## Content Emphasis Areas

These five pillars are woven throughout every module, not siloed into single lessons:

1. **Body Control** — How you hold yourself determines how others perceive you before you speak
2. **Active Listening** — Hearing what's said, what's not said, and what the room is feeling
3. **Improvisation** — Thinking on your feet without freezing, scripting, or over-preparing
4. **Reciprocation** — The give-and-take rhythm that builds trust and authentic influence
5. **Tonality & Presence** — Your voice, your timing, your energy — the invisible signals that command attention

## Lesson Structure (Standard Format)

Each lesson follows this 5-part structure (45–90 min total):

### 1. Concept Introduction (10–15 min)
- 2D animations explaining the principle
- AI-generated graphics/diagrams for visual clarity
- Research citations where applicable (neuroscience, psychology, communication studies)

### 2. Dance Connection (5–10 min)
- Dance lesson video demonstrating the concept physically
- Jon demonstrates the movement or principle in motion
- Clear connection drawn: "This is what [concept] looks like in your body"

### 3. Deep Dive (15–20 min)
- Detailed breakdown with real-world examples
- Case studies from coaching clients (anonymized)
- Before/after scenarios
- Framework or mental model introduced

### 4. Practice Exercise (10–15 min)
- Specific, actionable exercise the learner does on their own
- Can be movement-based, conversational, or reflective
- Clear success criteria: "You'll know this worked when..."

### 5. Reflection Prompt (5 min)
- Journal questions
- Self-assessment check-in
- Connection to previous and upcoming lessons

## Production Pipeline

### Video Recording

**Dance Lesson Videos (5–10 min each):**
- **Equipment:** DSLR or mirrorless camera (Sony A7IV or equivalent), lavalier mic, ring light or softbox
- **Setting:** Clean studio space with neutral background, good floor for movement
- **Wardrobe:** Professional-casual, on-brand colors (earth tones, minimal patterns)
- **Shot list per video:** Wide shot (full body), medium shot (torso up), close-up (hands/feet for detail)
- **Capture:** 4K 30fps, horizontal orientation

**Concept/Lecture Videos (10–20 min each):**
- **Format:** Direct-to-camera with B-roll cutaways
- **Setting:** Professional but warm — bookshelf background, natural light
- **Teleprompter or notes:** Keep natural, not scripted

### 2D Animations

**Tools:**
- **After Effects** — For complex motion graphics, data visualization, concept diagrams
- **Rive** — For interactive web animations (if embedding in lesson pages)
- **Lottie** — For lightweight animated icons and micro-interactions
- **Canva/Figma** — For static infographics and diagrams

**Style guide:**
- Match Zen aesthetic: clean lines, muted palette, generous whitespace
- Use brand colors: burnt indigo backgrounds, muted moss accents, rice paper textures
- Typography: Fraunces for titles, DM Sans for labels
- Minimal decoration — let the concept breathe

### AI-Generated Visuals

**Static Images:**
- **Midjourney / DALL-E** — Concept illustrations, metaphor visualizations, abstract mood images
- **Style prompt framework:** "minimalist editorial illustration, muted earth tones, Japanese zen inspired, professional, clean lines, [concept-specific prompt]"

**Motion/Video:**
- **Runway Gen-3 / Sora** — Supplemental motion graphics for concept intros
- Use sparingly — AI video quality is evolving; supplement, don't replace real content
- Best for: abstract visualizations, transitions, background textures

### Editing & Post-Production

**Tools:**
- **Descript** — AI-powered editing: transcript-based cuts, filler word removal, auto-captions
- **DaVinci Resolve** — Color correction, final export, advanced compositing
- **Subtitle workflow:** Auto-generate via Descript → review/correct → export SRT → embed in player

### Hosting & Delivery

**Option A: YouTube (Unlisted)** — Free, reliable, good player. Embed via `<VideoEmbed>` component. Risk: learner can share unlisted links.

**Option B: Mux or Cloudflare Stream** — Professional, DRM-capable, token-based access. Higher cost but prevents content sharing. Better UX (adaptive bitrate, no YouTube UI).

**Recommended:** Start with YouTube (unlisted) for speed, migrate to Mux when enrollment justifies the cost.
