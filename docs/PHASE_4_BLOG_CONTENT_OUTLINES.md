## Pillar 1: Physical Grounding

### Post: "Why Executive Presence Starts with How You Move"

**Target keyword**: executive presence body language, leadership posture

**Outline**:

1. **Intro Hook** (200 words)
   - Story: Meeting where quiet person stood up, entire room shifted attention
   - The science: 93% communication is non-verbal (myth-busting + real stats)
   - Thesis: "Physical grounding is the foundation of executive presence"

2. **Why Physical Grounding Matters** (300 words)
   - Defines grounding: connection to your body, stability, intentional movement
   - Brain science: nervous system reads body language first, then voice
   - Cost of being ungrounded: fidgeting, weak handshakes, appearing nervous
   - Opportunity: Intentional movement = perceived confidence

3. **The 3 Elements of Physical Grounding** (600 words)
   - **Posture**: Stand tall (spine aligned), shoulders back, chest open
     - Sub-section: How to check your posture right now
     - Common mistakes: locked knees, slouching, asymmetrical weight
   - **Stance**: Feet shoulder-width apart, weight distributed, balanced
     - Sub-section: Why stance matters in meetings/presentations
     - Example: US presidential candidates debate body language
   - **Movement**: Purposeful gestures, slow transitions, grounded transitions
     - Sub-section: The power of the pause
     - Example: How Steve Jobs walked on stage

4. **Quick Assessment** (200 words)
   - 5 questions: "Are you physically grounded?"
   - Link back to lead magnet (audit tool)
   - Teaser: "Take the full assessment to see your presence profile"

5. **Next Steps** (150 words)
   - "Physical grounding isn't innate—it's learned behavior"
   - Option 1: Free weekly lesson on embodiment
   - Option 2: 1:1 coaching (record yourself, get feedback)
   - Option 3: Group program (practice with others)
   - CTA to signup form

**Total word count**: ~1,650 words

**Formatting**:
- H2 for main sections
- H3 for subsections
- Bullet points for quick tips
- 1 embedded video (Jon doing posture demo)
- Internal link to Email 4 (framework intro)

## Pillar 4: Strategic Communication

### Post: "Say It Strategically: How to Communicate Like a Senior Leader"

**Target keyword**: executive communication, leadership communication strategies

**Outline**:

1. **Intro Hook** (200 words)
   - Story: Two executives with same idea; one promoted because of how they communicated it
   - The research: 70% of career progression is communication skills
   - Thesis: "Strategy is invisible unless you communicate it clearly"

2. **What Strategic Communication Means** (300 words)
   - Defines it: Clarity of message, audience awareness, alignment with business goals
   - NOT: Being a great talker or charismatic
   - Why it matters: Executives influence through communication
   - The challenge: Technical experts often communicate poorly

3. **The 3-Step Strategic Communication Framework** (600 words)
   - **Step 1: Clarity (Know Your Message)**
     - Sub-section: The 30-second version (elevator pitch)
     - Exercise: Write your key message in one sentence
     - Example: Bad vs. good message framing
   - **Step 2: Audience (Know Who You're Speaking To)**
     - Sub-section: Executive listeners want: data, impact, next steps (DIN framework)
     - Exercise: Tailor your message for C-suite vs. team vs. board
     - Example: Same idea, three presentations
   - **Step 3: Alignment (Connect to Business Goals)**
     - Sub-section: Frame your message as: business impact, timeline, resource needs
     - Exercise: The "Why this matters now" statement
     - Example: How alignment changed buy-in

4. **5 Strategic Communication Mistakes Quiet Leaders Make** (300 words)
   - Over-explaining (leading with complexity, not impact)
   - Burying the ask (waiting until the end to request something)
   - Apologizing for your idea ("This might be dumb, but...")
   - Using filler language ("Um," "Like," "You know")
   - Not asking for feedback or buy-in

5. **Practical Exercise: Reframe Your Next Pitch** (200 words)
   - Take a project/idea you're currently working on
   - Apply the 3-step framework:
     1. Message clarity
     2. Audience analysis
     3. Business alignment
   - Record yourself pitching it (video)
   - Self-assess: Does it land?

6. **Next Steps** (150 words)
   - "Strategic communication is a learned skill, not a talent"
   - Option 1: Free weekly communication workshop (30 minutes, live Q&A)
   - Option 2: 1:1 coaching (pitch your specific project, get feedback)
   - Option 3: Group program (practice in cohort, peer feedback)
   - CTA to signup form

**Total word count**: ~1,750 words

**Formatting**:
- Embedded video: Jon pitching the same idea badly, then strategically
- Downloadable: "DIN Framework Template" (PDF)
- Internal link: Email 4 (framework intro)
