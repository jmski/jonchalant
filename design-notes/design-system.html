01 / Foundation

Four principles, in quiet counterpoint.

The system is editorial, muted, unhurried. Warm and honest — not polished and aspirational. Every decision is filtered through four Japanese aesthetic pillars.

Ikigai

Purpose-driven

Every element earns its place. No filler. If you can't name what it's for, remove it.

Kaizen

Continuous refinement

Subtlety over spectacle. Hairline borders, small typographic shifts. Polish reads on second look.

Ma

Generous whitespace

The space between is the design. Sections breathe with a 4.5rem–11rem rhythm. Content columns stay narrow.

Wabi-sabi

Beauty in imperfection

Fraunces's WONK axis, cream not bone, an italic anchor word. Warm, never slick.

02 / Voice

Casual, direct, unpretentious. Specific over general.

Jon speaks like someone who's honest about tradeoffs and doesn't need to impress you. If a sentence could live in a LinkedIn thought-leadership post, rewrite it.

Do / Don't

Say this

  • "Most people are in the right industry. Wrong role."
  • "It's free. It takes ten minutes. If it tells you something useful, keep going."
  • "The medium changes. The fundamentals don't."
  • Headlines that make a claim, not a label.
  • CTAs that name what happens next: Discover Your Ikigai, Start the Course.

Avoid

  • Coaching jargon: unlock, transform, journey, empowered, authentic self, limiting beliefs, inner game.
  • Vague CTAs: Learn More, Get Started.
  • Stacked adjectives. Hype verbs. Exclamation marks.
  • Assuming the reader is a dancer.
  • Hiding the tradeoff. Name what it isn't.

Kinetic typography moments · phrase library

One per page, max. These are argument lines — they carry the page's claim, not decoration. Skip on functional pages (Blog, Lessons index, Contact, Portal).

05 / Four Pillars

The fundamentals are the same. The medium is yours to choose.

Grounding, Energy, Flow, Command. Any component rendering the pillars must carry at least three mini-applications across different mediums — this is the single strongest piece of proof that embodiment transfers.

Pillar 01

Grounding

The capacity to find your centre before you move.

  • A dancer finds it before the first step. Without it, every movement after is compensating.
  • A leader finds it before they speak. Without it, the room reads the uncertainty before the words land.
  • A writer finds it before the first sentence. Without it, the page fights back.
Pillar 02

Energy

Calibrating your presence to the room.

  • A performer reads the audience and adjusts — not performing harder, performing precisely.
  • A salesperson reads the client and adjusts — not louder, more matched.
  • Anyone managing their own output learns to spend energy where it moves the work, not where it feels busy.
Pillar 03

Flow

Moving between rigid structure and live response.

  • Dancers drill choreography until they can forget it. Then they can actually dance it.
  • Leaders prepare the message until they can leave it. Then they can actually have the conversation.
  • Professionals learn when to follow the plan and when to abandon it — in real time, without panicking.
Pillar 04

Command

Presence that carries authority without force.

  • Quiet command on stage. The dancer who doesn't have to chase attention.
  • Quiet command in a meeting. The person who speaks less and shifts the room more.
  • Quiet command in your own life. Deciding what you're here for, and behaving like someone who knows.
06 / Four Circles

Passion. Mission. Vocation. Profession.

Ikigai sits at the overlap of four things: what you love, what the world needs, what you can be paid for, and what you're good at. The quiz, the course, and the portal dashboard all present the circles in this order — always.

PassionWhat you love.
MissionWhat the world needs.
VocationWhat you're good at.
ProfessionWhat you can be paid for.

The Four Circles · 12-lesson curriculum

Lessons 01–04 are foundational. 05–08 get more challenging. 09–12 are the hardest and the most important — this is where the Foundation picks up. The course is designed so you can feel the difficulty increase.
